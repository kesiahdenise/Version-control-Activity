Aquatic Animal Characteristics

Sea Cucumber

A sea cucumber is a marine invertebrate in the class Holothuroidea, known for its soft, cylindrical body that resembles a cucumber, living on the seafloor worldwide. These echinoderms are distantly related to starfish and sea urchins, using tube feet to move and feeding on organic matter in the sediment.

Aquatic Animal Characteristics

Aquatic animals live in oceans, rivers, ponds, lakes, and streams. They get their oxygen from the water through their skin or gills. They have adapted unique features such as gills, fins, and streamlined bodies, allowing them to thrive in aquatic environments. Aquatic animals may live in freshwater or saltwater. Animals living in saltwater (oceans and seas) are called marine animals.

Examples of Aquatic Animals

Aquatic animals are diverse and include vertebrates or invertebrates, such as fish, whales, dolphins, crabs, and some reptiles. Some live their entire life cycles in water (fully aquatic), while others are semiaquatic, spending part of their time in water.

Some examples include:

  • Fish – there are many different types of fish including flounder, trout, and salmon.
  • Mollusks – small animals with soft bodies protected by a hard shell, including snails, clams, and oysters.
  • Crustaceans – animals with protective exoskeletons, including barnacles, crabs, lobsters, crayfish, and shrimp.
  • Marine mammals – these are air-breathing animals that live in water and include dolphins, seals, whales, and sea lions.
  • Reptiles – there are a few reptiles, such as water snakes and sea turtles, that live entirely in water.
  • Insects – many insects have a larval phase where they spend part of their life cycle in water
The Glaucus atlanticus, commonly known as the blue dragon, blue sea slug, or sea swallow, is a small, pelagic nudibranch (sea slug) that floats upside down on the ocean's surface. Found in tropical and temperate oceans worldwide, these 3-cm mollusks consume venomous creatures like the Portuguese man-o'-war and store the toxins for defense.
